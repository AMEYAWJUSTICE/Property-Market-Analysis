Analysis · Key Findings
Data Insights
📈
Strong Price Appreciation
Average sale prices rose from $715K in 2016 to $780K in 2017 — a 9.2% increase overall. Manhattan led with a remarkable +28.87% YoY gain, while all five boroughs posted positive growth.
🏆
Manhattan's Premium
Manhattan's average sale price of $6.57M is 6.6× higher than Brooklyn ($994K), the second most expensive borough. At $1,531/sqft, Manhattan's price per square foot is over 3× that of Brooklyn.
📊
Highly Skewed Distribution
With a skewness of 16.17, the sale price distribution is heavily right-skewed. The mean ($757K) is 28% above the median ($590K), indicating a small number of ultra-high-value sales pulling the average up significantly.
🏛️
Age Adds Value
Counterintuitively, older buildings command higher prices. Pre-1900 properties average $1,498,627, more than double the $644,996 average for post-1980 buildings. Historic character and prime locations drive this premium.
📅
Seasonal Transaction Patterns
Transaction volume peaked in June 2017 (2,354 sales) and hit its lowest in August 2017 (1,601). The market shows clear seasonality, with summer typically driving higher volumes at the end of the fiscal year cycle.
🌟
SoHo Leads Luxury Tier
SoHo tops neighborhood rankings at an average of $17.85M — 2.4× higher than the second-ranked Greenwich Village Central ($12.5M). The 1,166 luxury properties identified (2× borough avg) are concentrated almost entirely in Manhattan.
